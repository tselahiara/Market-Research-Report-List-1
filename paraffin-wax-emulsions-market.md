<p><p>Paraffin wax emulsions are classified primarily into non-ionic and ionic types. Non-ionic emulsions do not rely on charged particles and offer stable formulations suitable for various applications, including cosmetics and food coatings. Ionic wax emulsions, on the other hand, utilize charged emulsifiers and can be further divided into cationic and anionic types, enhancing specific performance characteristics like adhesion and stability in different environments. Both types serve diverse industries, including coatings, textiles, and personal care products.</p></p>
