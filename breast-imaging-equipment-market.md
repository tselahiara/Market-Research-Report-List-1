<p><p>The breast imaging equipment market comprises two primary types: analog and digital breast imaging equipment. Analog breast imaging uses conventional film-based techniques to capture mammograms, relying on x-ray technology to detect abnormalities. In contrast, digital breast imaging employs advanced digital sensors to produce high-resolution images, enhancing the accuracy and ease of interpretation. Digital systems also facilitate faster image processing and storage, making them increasingly popular in clinical settings due to their superior diagnostic capabilities and patient management efficiency.</p></p>
