<p><p>Recycled fiber fabric for apparel is made from two primary systems: cellulose and protein. The cellulose system derives fibers from plant-based materials, such as recycled cotton or wood pulp, which offer breathability and moisture-wicking properties. Conversely, the protein system utilizes fibers from animal sources, like recycled wool or silk, providing warmth and softness. Both systems contribute to sustainable fashion by reducing waste and resource consumption, appealing to eco-conscious consumers seeking environmentally friendly clothing options.</p></p>
