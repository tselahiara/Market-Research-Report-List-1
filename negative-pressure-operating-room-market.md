<p><p>A Negative Pressure Operating Room (NPOR) is a specialized surgical facility designed to maintain a lower pressure than the surrounding environment, thereby controlling the airflow and preventing contamination from airborne pathogens. These rooms are essential for procedures involving infectious patients or when sterile environments are critical. By ensuring that air flows inward from the surrounding areas, NPORs effectively minimize the risk of spreading infections, making them increasingly vital in modern healthcare settings.</p><p>The Negative Pressure Operating Room Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.7% during the forecast period, driven by rising concerns over infection control and the increasing prevalence of surgical procedures. Growing awareness about the importance of sterile environments and advancements in healthcare infrastructure are significant contributors to this growth. Additionally, the impact of recent global health crises has emphasized the necessity of such specialized facilities in hospitals. Emerging trends include the integration of advanced HVAC systems, real-time monitoring technologies, and automation in negative pressure environments. These innovations aim not only to enhance safety and efficacy in surgical procedures but also to comply with stringent regulatory guidelines, ensuring optimal patient outcomes.</p></p>
