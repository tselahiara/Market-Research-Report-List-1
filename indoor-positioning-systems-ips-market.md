<p><p>Indoor Positioning Systems (IPS) utilize various technologies to determine the location of objects or people within indoor environments. Radio waves, such as Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, provide location data based on signal strength and triangulation. Magnetic fields leverage unique patterns in magnetic flux to pinpoint locations. Acoustic signals use sound waves for positioning by measuring time delays between signals. Other market types may include visual systems utilizing cameras or beacons and sensor-based technologies, enhancing navigation and asset tracking indoors.</p></p>
