<p><p>The crude steel and iron market encompasses various production methods, primarily categorized into three types: the oxygen process, electric process, and others. The oxygen process, including the Basic Oxygen Furnace (BOF), uses molten iron and scrap metal, employing pure oxygen to convert iron into steel. The electric process, primarily through Electric Arc Furnaces (EAF), recycles scrap steel using high-temperature electric arcs. Other methods include Induction Furnaces and direct reduced iron techniques, each catering to specific production needs and resource availability.</p></p>
