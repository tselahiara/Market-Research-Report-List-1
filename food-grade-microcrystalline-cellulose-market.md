<p><p>The Food Grade Microcrystalline Cellulose market is categorized into two main types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from natural cellulose obtained from wood sources, providing bulk, texture, and stabilization in various food products. In contrast, refined cotton-based microcrystalline cellulose is sourced from cotton fibers, offering a higher purity level and often used in dietary supplements and food fortification. Both types enhance food quality and functionality through their unique properties.</p></p>
