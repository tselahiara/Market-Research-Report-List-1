<p><p>Heat Cost Allocators (HCAs) are devices used in multi-apartment buildings to measure and allocate heating costs based on individual unit consumption. Two main market types are evaporating style and electric heat cost allocators. Evaporating style allocators use a fluid that evaporates in proportion to the heat emitted by radiators, while electric heat cost allocators rely on sensors to measure surface temperature. Both technologies aim to promote energy efficiency by encouraging residents to monitor and manage their heating usage.</p></p>
